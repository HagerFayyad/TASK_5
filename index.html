YIN YANG

The Yin Yang symbol dates back to ancient Chinese philosophy founded by the Chinese sage Loazi in the 6th century BC and revolves around the idea of ​​the power behind everything in the universe or the eternal struggle between good and evil. It is considered a cultural symbol that expresses balance and harmony in all areas of life and also expresses the duality that forms all aspects of life and the universe and represents opposing but complementary forces.
YIN: symbolizes darkness, night and negativity.
YANG: symbolizes light, day and positivity.
The Yin Yang symbol shows this balance through a circle divided into two complementary parts: half black (yin) and half white (yang) with a small dot of white inside the black part and a small dot of black inside the white part. These small dots indicate that even at the extremes of yin there is something of yang and vice versa.
